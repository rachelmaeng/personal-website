ABOUT

Hi, I'm Rachel. I'm in Austin and I just finished at UT with a history degree!

I started college planning to go to med school, briefly thought about law school, and ended up studying history instead. Turns out I care way more about understanding how things work than I do about being a doctor or lawyer. I genuinely enjoy digging into archives and figuring out why systems function the way they do.

I'm Korean-American. My grandma makes her own soju, my mom has never made a Thanksgiving turkey, and I still can't make kimchi as well as either of them (working on it though). I grew up switching between languages and cultures, which probably shaped how I see things more than I realize.

Right now I work in marketing ops at a startup. I'm good at operational stuff - building systems, coordinating teams, making sure things don't break. There's something satisfying about when everything just runs smoothly. I'm looking for more work like that.

I love green (hence this entire site). I cook a lot because eating good food is honestly one of life's best things, I play the flute, and I fall into Wikipedia spirals way too often.

This site is where I archive things I'm thinking about, watching, reading, and making.
